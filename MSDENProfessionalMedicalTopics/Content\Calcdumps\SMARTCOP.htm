SMART COP Score for Pneumonia Severity

 
Blood pressure, CXR and nutrition factors

 Systolic BP <90 mmHg (2 points)

 Multiple lobes involved on chest x-ray (1 point)

 Albumin level less than 3.5 g/dL (1 point)
Respiration/Age factor

Any age: respiratory rate less than 25 breaths/minute (0 points)

 50 years old or younger: respiratory rate 25 breaths/minute or more (1 point)

 Over 50 years old: respiratory rate 30 breaths/minute or more (1 point)
Cardiovascular factor

 Tachycardia of 125 beats/minute or more (1 point)
Mental status factor

 Confusion (acute) (1 point)
Oxygenation/Age and acid-base factors

Any age: PaO2 ≥70 mmHg or O2 Saturation ≥94% or PaO2/FiO2 ≥333 if receiving O2 (0 points)

 50 years old or younger: PaO2 <70 mmHg or O2 Saturation ≤93% or PaO2/FiO2 <333 if receiving O2 (2 points)

 Over 50 years old: PaO2 <60 mmHg or O2 Saturation ≤90% or PaO2/FiO2 <250 if receiving O2 (2 points)

 pH (arterial) <7.35 (2 points)

 
Total Criteria Point Count:
 

 

 SMART COP Score and Risk for Ventilation or Vasopressor Support

 
0 to 2 points: Low risk
3 to 4 points: Moderate risk (approximately 12.5%)
5 to 6 points: High risk (approximately 33%)
7 to 11 points: Very high risk (approximately 67%)
